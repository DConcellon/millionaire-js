I — A1. INTRODUCTION / EXPLANATION

Today is a special day, today is “Game Show Day!” And today we’re playing a rendition of “Who Wants to Be a Millionaire?” It’s a UK game show that had its 20th anniversary just this May. I’ve tried my best to recreate this game for an authentic experience: some of the music is taken directly from the game show itself, the question format is the same, some of the graphics are the same, even. Hopefully it’ll be fun!

A2. FASTEST FINGER FIRST

1.1.        So, before starting off this game we need to pick out who will come here, on stage to play. (I bet at least 3 people will come to play.) To do so, just like on the show, we’ll play a round of “Fastest Finger First.” It’s a simple, quick game, and everyone here can participate. Here’s how it works:

1.2.        The person, who answers ONE question correctly, in the fastest time, wins. You will be asked to put ALL FOUR OF THE POSSIBLE CHOICES in the correct order. I will read out the question and those 4 choices, it will also be shown on screen, and you will have to submit it on your phone or tablet so that we can see who answered correctly first.

1.3.        Alright. Here’s an example. So, if the question was:

|         Put these dates in chronological order, starting with the earliest.

...and the possible answers were:

|         A: 1960                B: 1970                C: 2010                D: 1990

...you would have to enter ABDC. Because that’s the correct order, the numbers get bigger, pretty simple. The questions are going to be slightly harder than that. That’s how the knockout game works.

A3. MAIN GAME RULES EXPLANATION

2.1.        Now for the main game. The lucky contestant will have to answer 12 questions, each increasing in difficulty. So, for example, the first question will be very easy, but the fifth question will be significantly more difficult. And here’s an important thing: you must say “final answer”. Be careful — think about each choice, try to rule out which ones aren’t right. You get 30s to answer the question and, if you answer before the time runs out, you get to keep what you’ve saved, so the clock is accumulative.

2.2.        If you don’t know the answer, which is perfectly fine, you have 3 lifelines. These same 3 lifelines are also present on the current US game show on TV, so the experience is perfectly authentic. They are:

Plus One

50:50

Ask the Audience

When this lifeline is activated, your “Plus One” shall come to help you with their wisdom. Once the question is answered, your Plus One must go back to their seat.

There are 4 possible choices to each question. This lifeline removes 2 of 4 choices, leaving only 1 remaining correct and wrong answer. This gives you a 50% chance of answering right.

The audience will be asked to vote on the question on your phones & tablets, it’s just a poll. The results will be shown on screen in a percentage format (“A: 10%, B: 50%” and so on.)

2.3.        In addition, there are 2 safety nets. One at 5 pts, after answering the 2nd question, then another for the 7th question, which is for 10 points. If you take a shot and answer incorrectly after the first safety net, you’ll walk away with 5 points. Once you reach the second safety net, you will have 10 points guranteed.

2.4.        You can choose to walk away and keep what you have (be it 7 or 20 points). If the clock runs out of time, it’s the same as walking away, so you lose nothing. If you answer all 12 questions, you win!

2.5.        If you have any questions, go ahead and ask now, otherwise — let’s play!

III — C0. GAME OPERATOR INSTRUCTIONS

There are 3 windows for this game. Each will be covered in this manual.

Main game screen

This is the screen that shows the question, lifelines, clock, and is open when playing the game with the contestant.

Switching to this window:

Hold down Ctrl then click 1.

Interactive screen

This is the screen that shows audience voting results (but not the question). It should only be open after voting has finished.

Switching to this window:

Hold down Ctrl then click 2.

Intro & knockout game

This is the screen that the presenter uses when explaining the game’s rules or when playing Fastest Finger First.

Switching to this window:

Hold down Ctrl then click 3.

C1. OPERATING GAME

Most tasks from the main game screen can be performed by using the mouse. For other tasks…

1.1.        Starting a game.

         Hover over the top right corner of the screen until you see a box that says “Enter a number.”

        Click on the box and enter a number from  1  to    .

        If the number repeats in one lesson, the questions will be the same.

Make sure it’s different for each contestant.

1.2.        Using Ask the Audience.

        After clicking on the lifeline icon (it’s the 3rd from the left), wait until the music has finished.

        Once the music has finished, switch to the interactive screen, as noted in section C0.

        The interactive screen will show the graph. Wait until the contestant has seen the results and

        is ready to answer the question. The results can be seen by the host on their device.

1.3.        Quick action buttons.

⏸        The first icon pauses the clock.

🔄        The second icon restarts the clock.

⏩        The third icon shows the next question.

C2. OTHER NOTES

2.1.        The operator does not need to perform any actions for the introduction of the game and Fastest Finger First. However, the operator will need to switch to the main game screen once a contestant has been picked.

2.2.        Ideally, volume should not be changed during the course of the game. Background music is quieter than the sound effects (for example, the cue that plays once an answer has been picked). Background music should not be so quiet that it cannot be heard, but it should not be so loud that it’s hard to talk over it.

2.3.        After a contestant has finished playing, the page should be refreshed. To do that, press F5.
